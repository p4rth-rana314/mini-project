Rules:Gameplay
White moves first, then players alternate moves. A player wins by checkmating their opponent's king, or if their opponent resigns or runs out of time. A game is drawn if it ends in a stalemate, by agreement of both players, or if the 50-move rule is reached.
Time controls
Some competitions use time controls, such as 20 minutes with a 5-second delay. If a player exceeds their time limit, they lose the game unless they are checkmated.
Scoring
Players earn points for wins, draws, and losses. For example, a player might earn 2 points for a win, 1 point for a draw, and 0 points for a loss.
Tiebreakers
If players are tied in points, tiebreakers may be used to determine the winner. These tiebreakers may include the number of consecutive wins, total game wins, or fewest game losses.
Irregularities
If an incorrect starting position is discovered, the game is canceled and restarted. If an illegal move is made, the game returns to the position before the irregularity.
Player conduct
Players are not allowed to do anything that would bring the game into disrepute.
Arbiter
The arbiter ensures that the Laws of Chess are followed and makes decisions in the best interest of the competition.
